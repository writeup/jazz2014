How Tyler Yarema Found His Rhythm

Submitted by Erica Rae Chong on Thu Jun 28 2:46pm

By Erica Rae Chong

At 21, Tyler Yarema left his Thunder Bay hometown to seek stardom in the province’s biggest, glitziest city. He’d only started playing the piano five years earlier, but had his heart set on playing music for a living.

“The day was January 15 when I arrived in Toronto and it was so cold, everything was so dark, and the city seemed so huge,” recounted the 40-year-old musician who performs at the Reservoir Lounge this Thursday and Saturday. “There were nights of me thinking ‘How am I ever going to meet people?’ and ‘How am I going to get into this world?’”

George Benson: Timeless Talent 

Submitted by Erica Rae Chong on Wed Jun 27 4:51pm

By Erica Rae Chong 

In 1976, George Benson rocketed to fame with the pop-jazz album Breezin'. The title track's easygoing melody has survived the ensuing decades, becoming immortalised as a smooth jazz standard.

Thirty-six years on and the only things challenged by time are Benson’s thinning hair and bigger waistline. The 10-time Grammy Award winner, who turns 70 next year, proved he still has the chops, during a high energy showcase of skill and soul at the Toronto Star Stage Tuesday night.

Lady Kane: A Decade of Groove

Submitted by Erica Rae Chong on Sat Jun 23 12:20pm

By Erica Rae Chong

Keeping a band together is no easy feat, but Toronto-based sextet Lady Kane has managed to keep it going for 10 years, wowing audiences with their danceable covers of well-known pop, rock and soul tunes.

The group, comprised of singers Tracey Gallant, Ammoye Evans, Kerian Piper, guitarist Don Nevison, drummer Dave Peters and bassist/keyboardist Paul Antonio, returns to the TD Toronto Jazz Festival for two gigs (June 22 & 23 at 9.30 p.m. ) at its regular haunt Alleycatz.

The festival caught up with the band via Antonio, its official spokesperson.

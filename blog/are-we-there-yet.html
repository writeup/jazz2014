Are we there yet?

Submitted by Josh Grossman on Thu Mar 1 4:32pm

My brain kinda hurts. According to my very fancy spreadsheet, I'm now through 401 official submissions. And, when you add in all the other stuff I've been checking out, it's a lot of music. The problem is so much of it is good.

Let me qualify. I'm not disappointed to hear so much good music. I'm disappointed that so much of it I won't be able to feature at the festival. We're still figuring out how many slots exactly we have to fill, but even in the absolute best-case scenario we'll max out at around 45 spots - which means there will be a spot for about 10% of the submissions we've received. I'm not sure it's any harder this year than in past years to say no to people, but for some reason I'm not yet used to this part of the job. It's easy to say no to stuff that simply isn't a good fit musically, or doesn't meet the standard required to play at a major jazz festival. But splitting hairs - deciding why quintet A deserves a spot more than quintet B, for example, is tricky. And, as a colleague of mine in the business suggests (when we're commiserating inevitably over something bad for our health), it's especially tricky when we have to say no to a friend or musical collaborator.

All that said, it is an embarrassment of riches, in a way - I get to spend the next couple of weeks picking out the best from the submissions and making at least a few people happy. It's fun to see it all slowly coming together. In the meantime, though, until the lineup is fully announced in April, a couple of quick items:

If you're a musician who made a submission, I will do my best to be in touch whether or not you've got a spot in the festival. If for some reason I don't, though, please understand that I've simply been snowed under by the volume of submissions.

If you're a musician who has not yet made a submission, please don't call or email now and say things like "I think I'd make a great addition to the festival, and you'll have no trouble selling tickets to my show, and by the way here's my fee and I'll need eight hotel rooms too." I'm exaggerating. But only slightly. (I had one perturbing phone call this afternoon so I'm feeling a bit snarky.)

And, when I'm feeling a bit snarky, I close the door to my office, turn on the beats, and dance like this*:

*No I don't. But this video sure cheered me up. You see, I can barely dance at all, so I have a lot of respect for people who can dance like this. Plus, the reaction of the various teams to the outstanding moves of their soloists reminds me of the Duke Ellington band's reaction when Paul Gonsalves was hitting chorus number 20 (then 21 then 22...all the way to 27) in the middle of Diminuendo and Crescendo in Blue at the Newport Jazz Festival in 1956: pure elation, recognizing that something amazing had happened, was happening, would happen.

Kind of like booking a jazz festival. (What a segue!) And speaking of booking acts, we'll be announcing our next slate of acts on March 6, with the final lineup revealed on April 10. So stay tuned!

Josh

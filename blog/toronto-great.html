Toronto the Great?

Submitted by Josh Grossman on Thu Dec 13 4:24pm

In a few short weeks, I'll be heading down to New York City to check out the around-the-clock hive of activity associated with the annual APAP (Association of Performing Arts Presenters) conference. Every time I come back from New York I'm a bit stunned - by the metropolis itself, by the amount of incredible music on display, by the number of great musicians and venues - and it's difficult every time to not compare what happens there to what happens in Toronto. I sometimes even find myself discouraged - not that there isn't great music, and that there aren't great venues and musicians here...but why isn't there more happening in this city?

Well, for the first time in a while, I'm quite excited about what's happening here at home.

You may have seen this article in The Toronto Star last week about the soon-to-be-open Jazz Bistro. It will be living in a spot well-known to those familiar with jazz in Toronto - the building which once housed the Top of the Senator. This is great news for Toronto's jazz scene - there are lots of venues presenting jazz on a daily basis throughout the year, but few which are set up to feature top local, national and international jazz artists in a listening room. We're looking forward to the official opening date, which should be announced early in the new year.

In fact, there are number of new(ish) venues which are providing new performance opportunities, especially for those musicians with special projects on the go. I've just come back from lunch at the Paintbox Bistro, the brilliantly finished 120-seat restaurant/event space attached to the Daniels Spectrum in Regent Park. The Spectrum itself features the beautiful Ada Slaight Hall, a multi-purpose, completely flexible space with a capacity of up to 500 or so, and a smaller, excellent black box space run by Native Earth Performing Arts. And, just down the street, The Citadel, run by Coleman Lemieux & Compagnie, has been open for several months now and features a flexible performance hall for more intimate crowds of 60 or so. These venues are not perfect for every type of performance, but for the longest time I felt as though we had a dearth of smaller spaces in the city - and suddenly, there are far more options from which to choose.

As for the music-making happening around town, I've been very excited by many of the events which I've seen promoted recently on Facebook and in various other media. I've done my best to see a variety of shows, but it's perhaps the shows I didn't get to which are a good indication of the kind of interesting music happening these days in the city. In the past few weeks I was sorry to have missed:

  • The Music Gallery's presentation of Pheeroan akLaff and its follow up Black African Music Panel (which you can see here)
  • The release concert for the Mike Murley/Ed Bickert/Steve Wallace recording Test of Time
  • The release concert for Ken Aldcroft's Convergence Ensemble's Sneaky Pete - Slugs
  • This past weekend's Indo-Jazz Festival, produced by Justin Gray and Kay brothers Andrew and Jonathan
  • Last night's Feed it Forward, featuring a variety of great musicians in a fundraising event for the Daily Bread Food Bank

I know I missed some great shows...just the fact that these shows are happening speaks volumes to the health of the local scene.

On the festival front, we're slowly but surely assembling the lineup for the 2013 edition. We won't have any announcements for several more weeks yet, but what we will unveil early in January are the three selected TDJ Special Projects. And, based on the applications, they are sure to all be great shows, and a great celebration of local musicians.

So - are we on the cusp of a much-desired kick in the pants for the local jazz scene? I'm cautiously optimistic. But if the breadth of venues opening and music being made is any indication, we could be in for a fun couple of years. Of course, the best way to help invigorate a scene is to ensure that great shows have great audiences...so get out and see some live jazz!

Josh

P.S. - Whenever lists are assembled, as I've done here, there are bound to be omissions. If you know of a great new venue or want to mention a great show you've seen recently, please feel free to comment or drop me a line.

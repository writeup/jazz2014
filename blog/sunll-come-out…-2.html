The sun'll come out…

Submitted by Josh Grossman on Sun Jun 23 10:14am

So - did anyone notice that it rained yesterday? Yeesh…that was a lot of water falling from the sky. For the most part, I think, we were able to stay on schedule - thanks to all of the musicians for being good sports in sometimes challenging circumstances. All of our stages are covered, but that doesn't always matter when the rain is coming fast and furious like it did yesterday. Here's a rundown of my travels on the first full day of programming at this year's TD Toronto Jazz Festival:

  • 12:30 pm - I got to introduce Lighthouse on the Toronto Star Mainstage, kicking off the daily Big Band Series. The tent was full and the band was in fine, high-energy form. Especially impressive were the multi-part vocalizations!
  • 2:00 pm - Over to the Paintbox Bistro to introduce the Jazz for the Teach combo session. An enthusiastic (if somewhat drenched) group of teachers assembled to hear the teachings of Dave Neill and Shannon Butcher.
  • 3:00 pm - Off to the Distillery to catch some of the Shirantha Beddage Quartet and the Richard Whiteman Trio. As an added bonus, the sun finally came out. Oh - and - SOMA chocolate shop.
  • 4:00 pm - Back to Nathan Phillips Square to catch some of Samba Squad's roving activity. So good. I can't stand still when I hear this group, and it was a lot of fun to watch them parade through the square. Also - a great conversation with Walter Venafro from JAZZ.FM91.
  • 5:30 pm - I hopped in a cab to meet Dave and Shannon (of Jazz for the Teach fame) for dinner at The Burger's Priest. Not an official jazz festival venue, unfortunately. Great to catch up with two former classmates and, you know, burgers!
  • 8:00 pm - Mavis Staples on the Toronto Star Mainstage. It took I think only one note from Mavis to remind me of why she's so well-loved - deep, husky and so soulful.
  • 9:00 pm - A not-official-jazz-festival detour over to the Music Gallery to catch a few minutes of Matana Roberts' solo saxophone set. I wish I had been able to stay longer - I got the sense that she was taking the audience on an interesting compositional journey, and I only caught the first leg. I enjoyed what I heard.
  • 10:00 pm - Back to the Mainstage to hear some of Dr. John's New Orleans-infused set. I had not yet seen him live, so it was a treat to see in person the voice I had heard so often on the radio.
  • 11:00 pm - Over to the Jazz Bistro to catch Molly Johnson's Quintet. She sounded great, and I think this was an ideal setting for her. She seemed to have a lot of fun joking and interacting with her fellow musicians and also the audience. Plus - a lovely post-set chat.
  • 12:30 am - My last stop of the night was The Rex Hotel for Rudder. I enjoyed this set full of ballads and subtle acoustic tunes. Just kidding - this was an awesome kick-ass set of electric jazz-rock, a fun hang, and a satisfying way to wrap up the day.

But other than that, it was a quiet day. (I'm tired.) Here's what's up for today:

  • Martin Loomer & The Orange Devils as part of the Big Band Series - 12:30 pm, Nathan Phillips Square (free)
  • Harkness Herriot Duo - 12:00 pm, Pure Spirits Patio in the Distillery District (free)
  • JAZZ.FM91 Youth Big Band - 12:00 pm, Trinity Stage in the Distillery District (free)
  • Jazz for the Teach big band session with Gordon Foote - 2-5 pm, Paintbox Bistro (free, all welcome)
  • Rhythm & Truth - 3-6 pm, Nathan Phillips Square (free)
  • The Jivebombers - 3 pm, Shops at Don Mills (free)
  • Denis Keldie Trio - 3 pm, Pure Spirits Patio in the Distillery (free)
  • Terra Hazelton Trio - 3 pm, Trinity Stage in the Distillery District (free)
  • Courtney Pine opens up for John McLaughlin and the 4th Dimension - 8 pm, Toronto Star Mainstage
  • Ranee Lee - 8 pm, Jazz Bistro
  • James Cotton - 10 pm, Horseshoe Tavern

Plus all the activity in the clubs. Full details on today's activity are available here. I'll be offline for a few hours this afternoon as my big band (The Toronto Jazz Orchestra) rehearses for our performance this Wednesday at 12:30 as part of the Big Band Series - we're doing a tribute to Phil Nimmons!

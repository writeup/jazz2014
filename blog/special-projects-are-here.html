The Special Projects are Here!

Submitted by Josh Grossman on Thu Mar 29 4:43pm

It feels as though it was only last week that I was moderating a panel charged with selecting three TDJ Special Projects from a strong pool of applicants. It was in fact back in December...and with Spring now here, the first Special Project is only days away! And so - a bit about Easterween...

"A 500-year-old magician. A ruthless, multinational egg corporation. A love story between a Hasidic girl and an Amish boy. A band of sauciers and dumpling bakers. An egg-hunt of cosmic proportions to save us all from eternal winter.

Welcome to the world of Easterween."

Described as a cabaret–opera-styled song cycle, Easterween tells, in music composed by Juno-winning composer and bassist/cellist Andrew Downing, a story written by John Southworth, one of Canada's most interesting songwriters. With a seven-piece orchestra featuring some of Canada’s finest classical and jazz musicians backed by video projections by designer Yesim Tosuner, Easterween will transport listeners through a dark, Gothic Pennsylvania landscape, telling the magic-realist tale of a mythological egg hunt set during an eternal winter. It's a musical narrative about young love and its ability to save springtime from Darkness and evil.

It's hard to know exactly, based on the description above, what one might be getting oneself into with Easterween...but I know I'm intrigued. For some reason pictures of how the storyboards might look pop easily into my mind - maybe that's what makes Easterween so intriguing. The themes are common enough - good versus evil, protagonists with vastly different backgrounds, a search for a hidden treasure, "big bosses" versus "the little people"...but they are given a unique spin here.

It's the music that truly has me hooked. John acts as the sole singing narrator of the story, while the ensemble deftly illustrates the action, moving frequently between jazz and cabaret, classical and contemporary. I hear the bleakness of the landscape, but also the hope which comes with the youngsters and their quest; I hear distinctly Hasidic sounds but also the sound cues we can all relate to being "happy" or "sad". The arrangements are excellent - Andrew uses the unique instrumentation (clarinet, trumpet, trombone, violin, cello, guitar and bass) to maximum effect, often asking the players to extend their technique through the creative use of mutes, smears and plucks. It a beautiful story, composed brilliantly and performed exquisitely. From beginning to end, this should be an outstanding show.

Easterween runs Wednesday and Thursday, April 4 and 5, at the Lower Ossington Theatre, 100 Ossington Avenue. The show starts at 8 pm each night, and tickets are $15. Easterween is presented through a grant by the Canada Council for the Arts, in collaboration with Spectrum Composers Collective, Sud de Valeur records and The Ontario Arts Council. For more information on Easterween visit easterween.ca.

Josh

The other TDJ Special Projects are: The Thing Is CD Release on April 22 and 918 Bathurst, and the premiere of new music by Mike Downes' In The Current ensemble, May 9 at Gallery 345. For complete information visit our TDJ Special Projects page.
